These two methods to compute the volume
of a solid of revolution...
...have official names.
The first one,
when we cut the initial region
PERPENDICULAR to the axis of revolution,
is sometimes called the "washer method."
And the second one,
when we cut the initial region PARALLEL
to the axis of revolution,
is sometimes called
the "cylindrical shell method."
But I don't care about the names.
I even think that the methods
are basically the same:
cut the solid into infinitesimally
small pieces. That's it.
We just happen to cut in different ways.
Nevertheless, whatever you call them,
