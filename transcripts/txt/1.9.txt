The "theorem" I want to "prove"

This "proof" is wrong

The main problem

I began by ASSUMING

Effectively, I have shown

But THAT doesn't tell me

I have ONLY shown
that IF this inequality is true,

But THAT doesn't tell me
whether THIS inequality is true or not,

In general, it is forbidden to begin
by assuming what I want to prove.
I got it all backwards!
I should start ONLY
with things I already KNOW are true,
and END UP CONCLUDING this inequality.
In case this doesn't convince you,

what are x and y?
The statement of a theorem
is never JUST an inequality 
or an equation.
A theorem may tell us
WHEN an inequality is true,
or that it is ALWAYS true,
or that an equation has a solution;
but this is just a plain inequality,
nothing more.
And this is not me being pedantic,
because the inequality ISN'T always true.
For example, look at what happens

the left-hand side would be

1 ≤ -1? That would be FALSE.

However, I never NOTICED that

If this were a correct proof,

The goal of a proof is to figure out
whether a statement is true or not,

it is completely useless.
And finally, this proof has no words,
only algebra.
Sometimes,

EXPLAINING what we are doing.
