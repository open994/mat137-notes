A related rates problem is an application
of calculus to a physical situation
where we have multiple quantities that
depend on each other or on time,
and we need to find a relation between
their derivatives.
Here's an example of
a related rates problem.
A prisoner is trying to escape at night.
He runs in a straight line towards
the wall of the prison.
A guard points a spotlight
at the prisoner.
The spotlight is on the ground,
30m away from the wall.
The prisoner is 1.8m tall and
weighs 85kg.
How fast is the height of the shadow on
the wall changing
when the prisoner is 20m away from
the wall and running at 8m/s?

Even if you cannot fully solve it,

drawing a picture, understanding what it
is asking, and getting started,
my explanation will be much more useful.
First, we need to model the problem.
That is, we will take the word problem
and transform it into a math question.
I'll begin by drawing a picture.
Let's say that this is the
ground and here is the wall.
The prisoner is running towards the wall,
so let's put the prisoner maybe here,
and the spotlight is here.
And the light is pointing towards 
the prisoner,
and then it's producing
a shadow on the wall,
so all of this is going to be the shadow.
Now let's take the data from the statement
of the problem and add it to the picture.
First, we know that the distance between
the light and the wall is 30m.
I'm going to call this a,
and we know that a is 30m.
And we also know the height of the
prisoner; I'm going to call it h.
h is 1.8m, so this is not at scale.
And I know that these two quantities are
constant. They don't depend on time.
But I do have a few other quantities
that depend on time.
As the prisoner is running, the position
of the prisoner is going to change,
and the height of the shadow
is going to change as well.
In fact as the prisoner
gets closer to the wall,
I expect the height of the shadow to
decrease, to become smaller.
So, let's give names to these two things.
I'm going to call the height of the
shadow, which is this, y,
and I'm going to call the position of
the prisoner x,
and what I know is that x and y
depend on time.
Now what do we want to compute?
We want to find out how fast is the
height of the shadow on the wall changing.
That is, what I want is the derivative of
y with respect to t, with respect to time.
And I want to find this at a moment when -
let's see what's the data I have -
when the prisoner is 20m away from the
wall,

But since a was 30m,

The velocity of the prisoner is the

Now I need a relation between the

I can get one if I first get a relation

Luckily I can do that,

I have the triangle with

I can get the equation that says that h/x

Now that the problem is modelled, we can

I have a relation between x and y, and I
