To conclude the video, I want to
mention two important theorems.
First, there is an immediate
consequence of the
Least Upper Bound Principle,
which we already know.
The Least Upper Bound Principle
is about sets,
but we can extend it
to functions.
The theorem says that
IF a function is bounded above
on a domain that is non-empty,
THEN it has a supremum.
The second theorem is an
old friend we know very well:
the Extreme Value Theorem.
I remind you of what the
Extreme Value Theorem says:
If I have a function defined on a
closed and bounded interval [a, b],
IF the function is continuous,
THEN it has a maximum
and a minimum.
Notice that the two
theorems are related.
They both offer a
sufficient condition
that guarantees that a
function will have
a supremum,
in the first case,
or a maximum,
in the second case.
That is why I like to look
